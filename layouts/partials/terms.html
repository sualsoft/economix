Important terms

Level of requirements of the occupations

The occupation information is recorded according to the Classification of Occupations 2010. In addition to the exact job title, this also requires information on the level of requirements:

  1. Positions that usually do not require a formal vocational qualification or a maximum of one year of regular vocational training correspond to the level of "helper". This also includes civil servants in the simple service.

  2. Bodies that have in-depth specialist knowledge, which is usually acquired in the context of a 2- to 3-year vocational/technical school education, have the level of "specialist". This also includes civil servants in the middle service.

  3. Positions requiring complex activities, planning and control tasks, specialist knowledge and skills are assigned to the "Specialist" level. Usually, activities that require a master craftsman/technician training or a bachelor's degree, or civil servants in the higher service are included.

  4. Positions for highly specialized activities, e.g. in development, research or diagnosis, management and management tasks in large companies, which usually require at least 4 years of higher education (master's degree, diploma or state examination, etc.) or in which civil servants are employed in the higher service, correspond to the level "expert".

Employment Agency

This includes the local agencies of the Federal Employment Agency, the working groups between the Federal Agency and the municipalities (joint institutions, gE) and the municipal institutions for job placement (job center). Private employment agencies, on the other hand, are not one of them.

Employees

The number of employees includes all those working in your company: in addition to the employees and workers, also the owners/

-inside, the family workers, the marginally employed (e.g. mini-jobs) and the trainees. In the case of public authorities and state-owned enterprises, civil servants and judges must also be counted. There must be a direct employment contract with employees. Freelancers, employees on loan via temporary work, subsidised employment relationships (FAV) and one-euro jobs are not counted.

Notified body

A registered position includes unsubsidized employment relationships, which are registered by employers to the employment agencies and basic security institutions for placement (without seasonal jobs).

Temporary /temporary work

Temporary/temporary workers are hired by temporary employment companies and loaned to temporary employment companies for a limited time. They are therefore not employees or employees subject to social security contributions in the company in which they are employed for a limited period of time. An employment contract exists between the temporary employment agency and the borrowed person.
